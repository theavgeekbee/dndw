dndw: vibe coding

Welcome to dndw! A blog where you listen to a 15 year old ramble about coding like an old man.

wth is vibe coding?

Vibe coding is basically using AI to code your entire project. Basically, instead of being a programmer, you become a full time prompt engineer and instead of actual coding, you have AI do it for you. The idea is that it lets beginners code easier.

this sounds great! why are you complaining

Before you ask, no, I'm not secretly an 80-year-old. I have more reasons to dislike it, trust me.

The whole point of vibe coding is that it is helping beginners by making coding more accessible. It's also supposed to teach concepts that you would normally need a classroom to learn.

However, this is reaching very far here, and it usually never happens in real life.

what do you mean?

Let me take you on the journey of an individual on X, formerly known as Twitter, named Leo.

Leo is a non-technical person making a successfull SaaS (Software as a Service) using Cursor (an AI based IDE).

He successfully ships the app and then starts making money (yayyy!! 🎉).

Then, his app comes under a cyberattack. Weird requests are being spammed, usage limits are being reached.

After spending so much time figuring out how to rectify the situation, he ends up fixing it. Eventually though, he decides it was too much.

so what can we learn?

While you might be able to gaslight yourself into thinking you are learning, when push comes to shove, hackers can REALLY shove. Truth be told, cyberattacks don't really happen through ultra-clandestine zero-day vulnerabilities anymore (or at least, very infrequently).

When you code, you need to understand your framework. And if you don't bad things like this can be a huge headache.

You should only vibe code if you know what you're doing. You know how to prevent bad things from happening, and that will save a lot of debugging and headaches in the future. I myself use AI coding tools a lot, but when they get misused, bad things happen, especially when you're building in public.

tldr; vibe coding bad because you get cooked by one ddos attack
